A cytokine critical to both innate and adaptive immunity. Interferon gamma (IFN-γ) is a cytokine critical to both innate and adaptive immunity, and functions as the primary activator of macrophages, in addition to stimulating natural killer cells and neutrophils.

Cytokines....!

Cytokines are innate and adaptive immunity's molecular messengers that allow immune cells to communicate in a paracrine and autocrine way over short distances. As the immune system functions, both innate and adaptive components are involved to identify the stress encountered and to deliver an efficient response.

Interferons (IFNs)

Interferons (IFNs) are pleiotropic cytokines , belong to a family of proteins play an important role during innate and acquired immune response, with antiviral, antitumor, and immunomodulatory properties, being central coordinators of the immune response. The term “interferons” refers to the agents or molecules that interfere with viral replication and protect cells from viral infection.

There are three major types of IFNs. Type I IFNs (IFNa and IFNb), type II IFNs (IFNg), and the newly discovered type III IFNs, are classified according to their specificity to bind certain receptors. IFN-g can also promote host defense and immunopathologic processes and biologically active IFN-g is a small protein that exists as a 34-kDa homodimer. Its receptor is ubiquitously expressed on all nucleated cells.

Functions...!

A major function of IFN-g is to upregulate the MHC class I molecules, which aids in priming and presentation of antigens in the professional antigen-presenting cells. It was found that the serum of patients with COVID-19 has higher IFN-γ levels than that of healthy individuals and proposed that the elevation of this and other cytokines might result from the activation of Th1 and Th2 cells. elevated IFN-γ levels were associated with greater viral load and lung damage. IFN-γ consistently orchestrates both pro-tumorigenic and antitumorigenic immune responses in the tumor microenvironment (TME).
